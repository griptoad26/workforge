Why sideload first?

XKG is local-first software. The whole point is that your data stays on your devices, with no cloud, no telemetry, no tracking. Google's Play Store has policies that conflict with that:

  • Mandatory Play Services integration (we don't want it)
  • Background restrictions on apps that do peer-to-peer networking (our sync is P2P on the LAN)
  • Data safety disclosures that would misrepresent what we actually collect (nothing)
  • Forced use of Google Play Billing for in-app purchases (we use Stripe)

So we ship via direct APK download for v0.1.0 → v0.2.0, and revisit Play Store once the policies evolve (or once we ship a "Play-compatible" build that uses Google Pay).

Sideload vs Play Store — what you get

📦 Sideload (now)

  • No Google account required
  • No Play Services dependency
  • Pure APK, no bundled tracking SDKs
  • Auto-updates via cluster-hub check (optional)
  • Available right now

🛍 Play Store (Q4 2026)

  • Auto-updates through Play Store
  • Familiar install flow for non-technical users
  • Family Library sharing support
  • Sandboxed in Play Protect
  • Will require Play Services for IAP (otherwise doesn't get listed)

How to install the sideload APK

  1. Download the APKFrom the downloads page or directly: app-release.apk (22 MB).
  2. Allow your browser to install unknown appsSettings → Apps → Special access → Install unknown apps → select your browser → Allow. (Android 8.0+. On Android 7 and below, the toggle is under Security.)
  3. Open the APKTap the downloaded file in your notification shade or in the Downloads app.
  4. Confirm the installYou'll see the permission list (network, storage). Tap "Install".
  5. Open XKG MobileFrom the app drawer. First launch will ask for the cluster-hub URL.

Security: how to verify the APK

Before installing, you can verify the APK hasn't been tampered with:

$ sha256sum app-release.apk
e38c4c71cd0a02c1728255426cbdf4d01663b28fdd7a471e9118adf577a8dfe5

The expected SHA256 of the official v0.1.0 APK is e38c4c71cd0a02c1728255426cbdf4d01663b28fdd7a471e9118adf577a8dfe5. If your file's hash doesn't match, don't install it — re-download from the official release.

For extra paranoia, verify the APK signing certificate:

$ apksigner verify --print-certs app-release.apk

The expected signing certificate SHA-256 fingerprint is A1:B2:C3:... (full value in our RELEASE-SIGNING.md).
